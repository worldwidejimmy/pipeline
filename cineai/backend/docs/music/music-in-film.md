# Music in Film — Great Soundtracks and the Art of Film Music

## Two Approaches

Film music divides into two traditions: the original score (music composed specifically for the film) and the compiled soundtrack (pre-existing music selected and placed against image). Both approaches have produced masterworks; they require entirely different forms of creative judgment.

---

## Great Original Scores

### Bernard Herrmann (1911–1975)
The greatest film composer in the history of Hollywood. His collaborations with Alfred Hitchcock — Vertigo (1958), North by Northwest (1959), Psycho (1960), The Birds (1963, technically sound design rather than score) — created music inseparable from the films' emotional architecture. The Psycho string suite — 34 musicians playing only string instruments, creating the shower scene's stab of violence — is the most famous film music ever written. Herrmann also scored Citizen Kane (1941), Cape Fear (1962), Taxi Driver (1976, his final score, completed days before his death).

### Ennio Morricone (1928–2020)
The most prolific significant film composer in history, with over 500 scores. His collaborations with Sergio Leone — A Fistful of Dollars (1964), For a Few Dollars More (1965), The Good, the Bad and the Ugly (1966), Once Upon a Time in the West (1968), Once Upon a Time in America (1984) — created the sonic landscape of the Spaghetti Western. The Good, the Bad and the Ugly theme is the most recognized film theme in history. Won the Academy Honorary Award in 2007 and a competitive Oscar for The Hateful Eight (2015).

### John Williams (born 1932)
The most commercially successful film composer in history. Jaws (1975), Star Wars (1977), Close Encounters of the Third Kind (1977), Raiders of the Lost Ark (1981), E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial (1982), Schindler's List (1993), Saving Private Ryan (1998), Harry Potter (2001-2004). Williams' gift is for melody — themes that audiences can hum after a single hearing, that carry emotional weight on their own. His collaboration with Steven Spielberg, now spanning five decades, is the longest and most successful director-composer partnership in film history.

### Jonny Greenwood (born 1971)
The Radiohead guitarist has become one of the finest film composers of the contemporary era. There Will Be Blood (2007) — dissonant, percussion-heavy, using unconventional string techniques. The Master (2012) — jazz-inflected, unsettling. Phantom Thread (2017) — lavish orchestral romanticism. The Power of the Dog (2021) — banjo and chamber ensemble. Spencer (2021) — string quartet. His partnership with Paul Thomas Anderson is the most significant director-composer relationship of the 2010s.

### Trent Reznor and Atticus Ross
The Nine Inch Nails principal and his collaborative partner have become the definitive composers for David Fincher. The Social Network (2010, Academy Award) — electronic textures that suggest digital anxiety. The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo (2011) — industrial, severe. Gone Girl (2014) — ambient, deeply unsettling. Soul (2020, for Pixar). Their approach — layering electronic textures, avoiding conventional melody, using sound as texture rather than theme — has been widely imitated.

## The Compiled Soundtrack Tradition

### Apocalypse Now (1979)
Francis Ford Coppola's use of The Doors' "The End" — Morrison's death-obsessed psychedelia — over the opening napalm sequence established the compiled soundtrack as a form of artistic commentary. The Ride of the Valkyries sequence, playing Wagner from helicopter speakers over a village attack, is the most politically complex use of existing music in film history.

### Goodfellas (1990)
Martin Scorsese's use of pop music as historical document — the Copacabana sequence enters to The Crystals' "Then He Kissed Me"; the narrative moves chronologically through rock and roll history. The Layla sequence (using Derek and the Dominos' piano outro) over the discovery of bodies is one of cinema's most disturbing uses of music-as-irony.

### The Graduate (1967)
Mike Nichols' decision to use existing Simon & Garfunkel songs — The Sound of Silence, Scarborough Fair — rather than a conventional score established the model for using contemporary pop music in dramatic film. The use of The Sound of Silence over Benjamin's directionless face in the opening sequence created a template that hundreds of filmmakers have imitated.

### Almost Famous (2000)
Cameron Crowe's semi-autobiographical film about touring with a rock band in 1973 uses period music — Led Zeppelin, The Who, Elton John, Cat Stevens — as an emotional backbone. The Tiny Dancer bus scene, in which the fractured band finds momentary community singing along to Elton John, is the finest use of a pre-existing song in 21st century cinema.

### Pulp Fiction (1994)
Quentin Tarantino's use of surf rock (Dick Dale's Misirlou over the credits), soul (Urge Overkill's cover of Neil Diamond's Girl, You'll Be a Woman Soon), and pop (Al Green, Chuck Berry) created a sonic world as distinctive as the visual one. Tarantino's soundtracks — Natural Born Killers (1994, which he wrote), Jackie Brown (1997), Kill Bill (2003-2004) — established him as one of cinema's great musical curators.

### Wes Anderson
Anderson's use of music is among the most distinctive in contemporary cinema: British Invasion pop (The Kinks, The Rolling Stones) in Rushmore and The Royal Tenenbaums; French chanson (Jacques Dutronc) in The Darjeeling Limited; Alexandre Desplat's delicate original scores in The Grand Budapest Hotel and The French Dispatch. Anderson treats music as another designed element of his precisely controlled aesthetic worlds.
