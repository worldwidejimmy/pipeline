# Radiohead — The Most Important Rock Band of the Last 30 Years

## Formation and Members

Radiohead formed in Abingdon, Oxfordshire, England in 1985 under the name "On a Friday" — they rehearsed on Fridays at Abingdon School. The members met as students: Thom Yorke (lead vocals, guitar, piano, keyboards), Jonny Greenwood (lead guitar, keyboards, synthesizers, orchestration), Colin Greenwood (bass), Ed O'Brien (guitar, backing vocals), and Philip Selway (drums, percussion). They renamed themselves Radiohead in 1991, taking the name from a Talking Heads song. Signed to Parlophone in 1991, released their debut single "Creep" in 1992.

## Discography

### Pablo Honey (1993)
Their debut album, dominated by the unexpected worldwide success of "Creep" — a song about self-loathing and inadequacy that became an unlikely anthem. The band largely disowned the album's sound, finding its grunge-adjacent rock limiting.

### The Bends (1995)
A massive leap forward. The Bends established Radiohead as a serious band: Just, Fake Plastic Trees, High and Dry, Street Spirit (Fade Out). Thom Yorke's voice — capable of extraordinary tenderness and extraordinary despair — became one of rock's defining instruments.

### OK Computer (1997)
Widely considered one of the greatest albums ever made. Recorded in a country house in Bath, OK Computer addressed technological alienation, consumerism, and political exhaustion with a musical ambition that synthesized art rock, orchestral pop, and electronic texture. Paranoid Android, Karma Police, Exit Music (for a Film), No Surprises, Let Down, Subterranean Homesick Alien. Jonny Greenwood's guitar work — dissonant, searching, capable of sudden lyrical beauty — is the album's sonic spine. Won the Grammy for Best Alternative Album.

### Kid A (2000)
A radical departure. Abandoning conventional guitar-rock entirely, Radiohead made an album of electronic textures, jazz-influenced drums, processed voices, and abstract orchestration. Everything in Its Right Place, How to Disappear Completely, Idioteque, Motion Picture Soundtrack. The album divided fans and critics on release; it is now considered one of the most significant artistic pivots in rock history. It demonstrated that a major rock band could reinvent itself completely without losing its audience.

### Amnesiac (2001)
Recorded during the Kid A sessions, Amnesiac has a more acoustic character than its predecessor. Pyramid Song, Knives Out, Dollars and Cents, Life in a Glasshouse (with Humphrey Lyttelton's jazz band).

### Hail to the Thief (2003)
A return to more conventional rock structures, but filtered through the electronic textures of Kid A and Amnesiac. 2+2=5, Sit Down Stand Up, There There, The Gloaming. The album was mixed quickly by Radiohead's standards and felt, to some, transitional.

### In Rainbows (2007)
Released directly to fans via a pay-what-you-want model — a landmark moment in the music industry's digital transition. Musically, In Rainbows is Radiohead's most intimate and warmly produced album. Bodysnatchers, Weird Fishes/Arpeggi, All I Need, Reckoner, House of Cards, Videotape. Won the Grammy for Best Alternative Album.

### The King of Limbs (2011)
Their most minimal album — eight tracks, dominated by processed beats and layers of texture. Bloom, Morning Mr Magpie, Lotus Flower, Codex, Give Up the Ghost.

### A Moon Shaped Pool (2016)
Their most orchestral album, with string arrangements by Jonny Greenwood. Burn the Witch (featuring a Wicker Man-inspired video), Daydreaming, True Love Waits (a live staple since the 1990s, finally studio-recorded). The album was released following the end of Thom Yorke's 23-year relationship with Rachel Owen, who died of cancer in 2016, giving it an elegiac quality.

## Jonny Greenwood and Film

Jonny Greenwood is one of the finest film composers working today. His film scores include:
- **There Will Be Blood** (2007, dir. Paul Thomas Anderson) — dissonant, visceral, using strings and percussion in unconventional ways
- **The Master** (2012, dir. Paul Thomas Anderson) — jazz-inflected, featuring saxophone
- **Inherent Vice** (2014, dir. Paul Thomas Anderson)
- **Phantom Thread** (2017, dir. Paul Thomas Anderson) — lush orchestral score
- **Spencer** (2021, dir. Pablo Larraín) — chamber music for Princess Diana's story
- **The Power of the Dog** (2021, dir. Jane Campion) — banjo and strings
- **Licorice Pizza** (2021, dir. Paul Thomas Anderson)

Greenwood's partnership with Paul Thomas Anderson is one of the great director-composer collaborations in contemporary cinema.

## Thom Yorke Solo and Atoms for Peace

Thom Yorke's solo work includes The Eraser (2006), Tomorrow's Modern Boxes (2014), and Anima (2019, accompanied by a short film directed by Paul Thomas Anderson). He is also a member of Atoms for Peace with Flea (Red Hot Chili Peppers), and has scored films including Suspiria (2018, dir. Luca Guadagnino).

---

## Legacy

Radiohead's willingness to reinvent themselves — from the guitar rock of The Bends to the electronic abstraction of Kid A to the orchestral warmth of A Moon Shaped Pool — while maintaining a consistent emotional integrity makes them unique in rock history. Their influence on alternative music from the late 1990s onward is incalculable: the melancholy electronic sound they established on Kid A can be heard in Bon Iver, James Blake, Alt-J, and dozens of others.
