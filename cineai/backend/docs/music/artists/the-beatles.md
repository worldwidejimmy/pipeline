# The Beatles — The Most Important Band in Music History

## Formation and Members

The Beatles formed in Liverpool, England in 1960. The classic lineup: John Lennon (rhythm guitar, vocals), Paul McCartney (bass, vocals), George Harrison (lead guitar, vocals), and Ringo Starr (drums, vocals). Their manager Brian Epstein discovered them playing at the Cavern Club in Liverpool in 1961; their producer George Martin signed them to Parlophone and shaped their studio sound throughout their career.

The band had an earlier lineup that included Pete Best (drums, replaced by Ringo Starr in 1962) and Stuart Sutcliffe (bass, died 1962). John Lennon and Paul McCartney met in 1957 when Lennon's skiffle group The Quarrymen played at a church fete — their songwriting partnership, formed that day, produced the most successful catalogue of popular songs in history.

## Career and Albums

### Early Period (1963–1964)
Please Please Me (1963) — recorded in a single day. With the Beatles (1963). A Hard Day's Night (1964) — their film debut, directed by Richard Lester, is the finest rock film ever made. Beatles for Sale (1964). During this period the Beatles conquered Britain and then America — their appearance on The Ed Sullivan Show on February 9, 1964 was watched by 73 million Americans, a third of the country's population.

### The Middle Period (1965–1966)
Help! (1965) — another film, more psychologically raw songs (Lennon's You've Got to Hide Your Love Away, Yesterday by McCartney). Rubber Soul (1965) — their first fully mature album, drawing on folk, soul, and Indian influences. Revolver (1966) — the album that anticipated everything. Tomorrow Never Knows, Eleanor Rigby, Here, There and Everywhere, Yellow Submarine, Taxman — a record that sounds unlike anything that came before and contains the seeds of virtually everything that came after. The Beatles stopped touring after 1966 — unable to hear themselves over screaming crowds — and became exclusively a studio band.

### The Apex (1967–1968)
Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band (1967) — the most influential album in rock history, the concept album that proved the album could be a unified work of art rather than a collection of singles. A Day in the Life. Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds. With a Little Help from My Friends. The album occupied the number one position in the UK for 23 weeks. Magical Mystery Tour (1967). The White Album (The Beatles, 1968) — 30 songs, ranging from Back in the USSR to Blackbird to Helter Skelter to Julia, representing the full range of what rock could contain.

### The Final Period (1968–1970)
Yellow Submarine (1968, film and soundtrack). Abbey Road (1969) — their final recorded album, the long medley on Side Two stitching together fragments into one of rock's greatest sustained statements. Let It Be (1970) — their final released album, recorded before Abbey Road during sessions for a documentary film. The band broke up in 1970, partly due to business disagreements following Brian Epstein's death in 1967, partly due to the growing tensions between Lennon and McCartney, partly due to the integration of Yoko Ono and Linda Eastman into the group's dynamics.

## The Lennon-McCartney Partnership

John Lennon and Paul McCartney wrote together and separately throughout the Beatles' career, but credited all compositions jointly as Lennon-McCartney. In practice, most songs were primarily by one or the other: Lennon's songs tend toward introspection, confrontation, and surrealism (Strawberry Fields Forever, I Am the Walrus, Come Together); McCartney's toward melody, craft, and optimism (Yesterday, Hey Jude, Let It Be, Blackbird). Their competition and collaboration produced something neither could have achieved alone.

---

## Solo Careers

After the breakup, all four pursued solo careers. Lennon's Imagine (1971) and John Lennon/Plastic Ono Band (1970) are among the finest solo work. McCartney's Ram (1971) and Band on the Run (1973) established him as the most commercially successful of the four. Harrison's All Things Must Pass (1970) is often cited as the finest Beatles solo album. Ringo Starr had a period of significant commercial success in the early 1970s. John Lennon was shot and killed in New York City on December 8, 1980. George Harrison died of lung cancer on November 29, 2001.

---

## Influence

The Beatles' influence on popular music is incalculable. They established that rock could be art, that the album was a compositional form, that popular music could absorb influences from anywhere (Indian classical music, musique concrète, music hall) without losing its popular appeal. Every major rock act of the subsequent half-century has engaged with their legacy.
