# Simon & Garfunkel and Paul Simon

## Simon & Garfunkel

Paul Simon (born 1941, Newark, New Jersey) and Art Garfunkel (born 1941, Forest Hills, Queens) met as children in Queens, New York, and began performing together as teenagers under the name Tom & Jerry. They signed to Columbia Records as Simon & Garfunkel in 1964.

### Discography

**Wednesday Morning, 3 A.M.** (1964) — their debut, folk-oriented, commercially unsuccessful.

**Sounds of Silence** (1966) — The Sound of Silence, originally recorded acoustically on Wednesday Morning, 3 A.M., was overdubbed with electric guitar, bass, and drums by Columbia (without the duo's knowledge) and became a number one single in 1965. The album built around the hit established their commercial viability.

**Parsley, Sage, Rosemary and Thyme** (1966) — Scarborough Fair/Canticle (an anti-war descant against the traditional folk song), The 59th Street Bridge Song (Feelin' Groovy), A Simple Desultory Philippic. Their first album to chart highly.

**Bookends** (1968) — America, Mrs. Robinson (written for The Graduate), Hazy Shade of Winter, At the Zoo, Old Friends. A concept album of sorts, exploring aging and disillusionment. Mrs. Robinson was written for Mike Nichols' The Graduate (1967) and won the Grammy for Record of the Year.

**Bridge Over Troubled Water** (1970) — their most successful album. Bridge Over Troubled Water (Art Garfunkel's vocal showcase, one of the most celebrated performances in popular music), The Boxer (Paul Simon's finest lyric), Cecilia, El Condor Pasa (If I Could), Keep the Customer Satisfied. Five Grammy Awards. The duo broke up immediately after its completion — Simon felt creatively constrained by the duo format, and tensions over Garfunkel's acting career (he appeared in Catch-22 and Carnal Knowledge) had strained the partnership.

### The Graduate (1967)
The Simon & Garfunkel soundtrack to Mike Nichols' The Graduate — featuring The Sound of Silence, Scarborough Fair/Canticle, Mrs. Robinson, and April Come She Will — is one of the most influential film soundtracks in cinema history, establishing the model of using existing popular music as a film score rather than commissioning original compositions.

## Paul Simon Solo

After the breakup, Paul Simon became one of the finest solo artists of his generation.

**Paul Simon** (1972) — Me and Julio Down by the Schoolyard, Mother and Child Reunion (recorded in Jamaica, one of the first American rock records with reggae rhythm section), Duncan.

**There Goes Rhymin' Simon** (1973) — Kodachrome, Loves Me Like a Rock (with the Dixie Hummingbirds), American Tune.

**Still Crazy After All These Years** (1975) — 50 Ways to Leave Your Lover, Still Crazy After All These Years, My Little Town (recorded with Art Garfunkel). Won Album of the Year Grammy.

**One-Trick Pony** (1980) — film and soundtrack. Late in the Evening.

**Hearts and Bones** (1983) — underperformed commercially but contains some of Simon's finest writing.

**Graceland** (1986) — Simon traveled to South Africa and recorded with Black South African musicians, creating an album that synthesized mbaqanga, zydeco, and American rock. Graceland, You Can Call Me Al, Diamonds on the Soles of Her Shoes, The Boy in the Bubble. The album was controversial (South Africa was under apartheid; Simon was accused of violating the cultural boycott) and commercially triumphant. One of the most significant albums of the 1980s. Won Album of the Year Grammy.

**The Rhythm of the Saints** (1990) — recorded with Brazilian musicians. The Obvious Child.

**You're the One** (2000), **Surprise** (2006, with Brian Eno as producer), **So Beautiful or So What** (2011), **Stranger to Stranger** (2016), **In the Blue Light** (2018, orchestral reworkings of earlier material).

**Seven Psalms** (2023) — a 33-minute song cycle on mortality, recorded after Simon experienced sudden hearing loss. One of the most moving final statements in popular music.

## Songs in Film and Television

Simon & Garfunkel's music has been extensively used in film and television:
- **The Sound of Silence** — The Graduate (1967), Old Boy (2003), Kingsman: The Secret Service (2015)
- **Mrs. Robinson** — The Graduate (1967)
- **The Boxer** — multiple documentaries, Arrival references its line "I am just a poor boy"
- **Scarborough Fair** — The Graduate (1967), numerous other films
- **America** — Almost Famous (2000)
- **Kodachrome (Paul Simon)** — used extensively in period pieces set in the 1970s
- **You Can Call Me Al (Paul Simon)** — The Royal Tenenbaums (2001)
- **The 59th Street Bridge Song (Feelin' Groovy)** — used in various comedy films

Paul Simon and Art Garfunkel reunited for a concert in Central Park in 1981 (attended by approximately 500,000 people), for their 2003-2004 Old Friends Tour, and have performed together occasionally since.
