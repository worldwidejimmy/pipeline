# The Best Films of the 2000s

## The Decade Defined
The 2000s opened with two cultural events that reshaped cinema: the September 11 attacks changed the tone of American action and war films, and Peter Jackson's Lord of the Rings trilogy demonstrated that fantasy epics were commercially viable at a scale studios had not previously attempted. The decade saw the rise of Christopher Nolan, the dominance of superhero cinema (beginning with Spider-Man in 2002), the full flowering of Asian cinema in the West, and the last great decade of adult-oriented prestige drama before streaming fragmented the audience.

## The Epic Fantasies
- **The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring (2001)** — Rating: 8.8. Peter Jackson. The most ambitious fantasy adaptation ever attempted. Succeeded completely.
- **The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers (2002)** — Rating: 8.8.
- **The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King (2003)** — Rating: 9.0. Won 11 Oscars. The greatest fantasy trilogy ever made.
- **Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (2004)** — Rating: 7.9. Alfonso Cuarón's instalment is the best of the series by some distance.

## Christopher Nolan's Rise
- **Memento (2000)** — Rating: 8.4. Told backwards. A man with no short-term memory investigates his wife's murder. A puzzle film with genuine emotional stakes.
- **Insomnia (2002)** — Rating: 7.2. Al Pacino as a detective in the Alaskan midnight sun.
- **Batman Begins (2005)** — Rating: 8.3. Reinvented the superhero film as serious drama.
- **The Prestige (2006)** — Rating: 8.5. Two rival magicians. A film about obsession and the price of greatness.
- **The Dark Knight (2008)** — Rating: 9.0. Heath Ledger's Joker. The best superhero film ever made.

## Drama and Crime
- **Mulholland Drive (2001)** — Rating: 7.9. David Lynch. Hollywood dreams and nightmares. Naomi Watts. The film resists interpretation and is richer for it.
- **City of God (2002)** — Rating: 8.6. Fernando Meirelles. The rise of organised crime in Rio de Janeiro. One of the most kinetically directed films of the decade.
- **25th Hour (2002)** — Rating: 7.7. Spike Lee. Edward Norton's last day before prison. One of the few films to honestly process 9/11.
- **Mystic River (2003)** — Rating: 7.9. Clint Eastwood. Three childhood friends, a murder, and the past that never lets go.
- **Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind (2004)** — Rating: 8.3. Michel Gondry / Charlie Kaufman. A man has his ex-girlfriend erased from his memory. The most original love story in a decade.
- **Crash (2004)** — Rating: 7.7. Paul Haggis. Won Best Picture. Still controversial. A film about race relations in Los Angeles that pleased almost no one for long.
- **Brokeback Mountain (2005)** — Rating: 7.7. Ang Lee. A decades-long love story between two cowboys in Wyoming. Should have won Best Picture.
- **There Will Be Blood (2007)** — Rating: 8.2. Paul Thomas Anderson. Daniel Day-Lewis as an oil prospector. "I drink your milkshake." The greatest American performance of the decade.
- **No Country for Old Men (2007)** — Rating: 8.2. The Coen Brothers. Anton Chigurh. The coin flip. A film about what happens when evil does not play by the rules.
- **The Dark Knight (2008)** — Rating: 9.0.
- **Slumdog Millionaire (2008)** — Rating: 8.0. Danny Boyle. A Mumbai slum kid on Who Wants to Be a Millionaire. Won Best Picture; still argued about.
- **Inglourious Basterds (2009)** — Rating: 8.4. Tarantino's WWII revisionist fantasy.

## Science Fiction and Fantasy
- **Minority Report (2002)** — Rating: 7.7. Spielberg. Pre-crime and the ethics of surveillance.
- **Children of Men (2006)** — Rating: 7.9. Alfonso Cuarón. The last woman to get pregnant in an infertile world. The single-take action sequences. Clive Owen.
- **Pan's Labyrinth (2006)** — Rating: 8.2. Guillermo del Toro. A girl in fascist Spain retreats into a fairy tale. Simultaneously beautiful and horrifying.
- **Wall-E (2008)** — Rating: 8.4. Pixar. A robot alone on a destroyed Earth. The first 40 minutes contain almost no dialogue and are among the most beautiful things Pixar has made.
- **District 9 (2009)** — Rating: 7.9. Neill Blomkamp. Apartheid allegory as alien invasion. Shot as a documentary. Made for $30 million.
- **Avatar (2009)** — Rating: 7.9. James Cameron. The highest-grossing film ever at the time. Technically revolutionary. Narratively conventional. An experience in 3D.

## International Cinema
- **Spirited Away (2001)** — Rating: 8.6. Hayao Miyazaki. Studio Ghibli. A girl trapped in a spirit world. The greatest animated film of the decade.
- **Amelie (2001)** — Rating: 8.3. Jean-Pierre Jeunet. A shy Parisian waitress improves the lives of everyone around her.
- **Oldboy (2003)** — Rating: 8.4. Park Chan-wook. A man imprisoned for 15 years hunts his captor.
- **House of Flying Daggers (2004)** — Rating: 7.6. Zhang Yimou. Wuxia film as pure visual poetry.
- **Caché (2005)** — Rating: 7.3. Michael Haneke. A couple receive surveillance tapes of their own house. Deliberately irresolvable.
- **The Lives of Others (2006)** — Rating: 8.4. A Stasi officer in East Germany begins to identify with the people he's surveilling. Won Best Foreign Language Film.
- **4 Months, 3 Weeks and 2 Days (2007)** — Rating: 7.9. Cristian Mungiu. An illegal abortion in Ceaușescu's Romania. Devastating.
- **Let the Right One In (2008)** — Rating: 8.0. Swedish vampire film. A bullied boy befriends a vampire child. The best horror film of the decade.

## For a 2000s Film Festival
**The Epics:** Lord of the Rings trilogy, The Dark Knight
**The Art House:** Eternal Sunshine, There Will Be Blood, No Country for Old Men, Mulholland Drive
**International:** Spirited Away, City of God, Oldboy, The Lives of Others
