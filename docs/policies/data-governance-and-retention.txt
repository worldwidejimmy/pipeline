DATA GOVERNANCE AND RETENTION POLICY
Meridian Data Corp
Version: 2.1
Effective Date: 2025-01-01
Owner: Data Governance Committee
Last Review: 2025-09-15

==============================================================
1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
==============================================================

This policy defines the standards and requirements for data classification,
retention, access control, and disposal at Meridian Data Corp. It applies
to all employees, contractors, and third-party vendors who access, process,
or store Meridian data.

==============================================================
2. DATA CLASSIFICATION
==============================================================

2.1 Classification Levels

LEVEL 1 — PUBLIC
Data intended for public disclosure. No restrictions on sharing.
Examples: press releases, public API documentation, job postings.

LEVEL 2 — INTERNAL
Data intended for internal use only. Not for public disclosure but
not specifically sensitive.
Examples: internal wikis, process documentation, org charts,
engineering runbooks, meeting notes.

LEVEL 3 — CONFIDENTIAL
Sensitive business data. Access on a need-to-know basis.
Examples: financial reports, customer contracts, product roadmaps,
partner agreements, employee performance reviews.

LEVEL 4 — RESTRICTED
Highly sensitive data. Strictly controlled. Requires explicit
authorization and audit trail.
Examples: PII (personally identifiable information), payment card data,
credentials and secrets, M&A information, legal hold documents,
MNPI (material non-public information), security vulnerabilities.

2.2 Default Classification

When in doubt, classify data as CONFIDENTIAL (Level 3) until
a determination can be made. Do not classify down without approval
from the data owner.

2.3 Data Owner Responsibilities

Each dataset must have a designated Data Owner responsible for:
- Assigning and maintaining data classification
- Approving access requests beyond default group permissions
- Reviewing and approving data sharing with third parties
- Ensuring retention schedules are applied

==============================================================
3. DATA RETENTION SCHEDULES
==============================================================

3.1 Retention by Data Type

Customer PII and Account Data:
- Active account data: Retained for duration of account + 2 years
- Deleted account data: Purged within 30 days of deletion request (GDPR/CCPA)
- Transaction records: 7 years (financial regulation)

Engineering Data:
- Production logs: 90 days (Elasticsearch/Kibana rolling retention)
- Application metrics (Prometheus): 15 days hot, 1 year in Thanos long-term storage
- Build artifacts (Docker images, binaries): 1 year or until superseded
- Git history: Indefinite
- Incident records and postmortems: 5 years
- Security scan results: 2 years

ML/AI Data:
- Training datasets: 3 years or until model is decommissioned + 1 year
- Model artifacts and weights: Duration of model production use + 2 years
- Inference logs (for debugging): 30 days
- Evaluation datasets: 3 years
- Embedding vectors in production vector stores: Duration of pipeline use + 6 months

Business Data:
- Financial records: 7 years (IRS requirements)
- Employee records: Duration of employment + 7 years
- Legal hold documents: Until hold is released + 1 year
- Email communications: 3 years
- Contracts: 10 years after expiration

3.2 Retention System

Automated retention enforcement is managed through:
- Elasticsearch: ILM (Index Lifecycle Management) policies, configured per index
- S3/GCS: Object lifecycle rules, automated via Terraform
- PostgreSQL: Partitioned tables with automated partition drops (managed by DBA team)
- Milvus/Vector stores: Manual collection archival — ML Platform team owns schedule

Data owners are responsible for registering datasets with the Data Catalog
(accessible at catalog.internal) and tagging with the appropriate retention policy.

==============================================================
4. ACCESS CONTROL
==============================================================

4.1 Principles

Least Privilege: Users and systems should be granted only the access
required for their specific function.

Need-to-Know: Level 3 and Level 4 data access requires a documented
business justification.

Separation of Duties: No single individual should have unchecked access
to create, approve, and audit sensitive data changes.

4.2 Access Groups

All data access is managed through Okta groups. Access requests are
submitted through the IT Service Portal and require manager approval
for Level 3 data and manager + Data Owner approval for Level 4 data.

Standard groups:
- engineering-all: Read access to internal documentation, runbooks (Level 2)
- ml-platform: Access to ML datasets and model artifacts (Level 3)
- data-platform-ops: Read/write to production data pipelines (Level 3)
- dba-team: Database admin access (Level 4 gated)
- secops: Security tooling and vulnerability data (Level 4)
- finance-team: Financial data access (Level 4)

4.3 Service Account Access

Service accounts (used by automated systems, CI/CD, and APIs) must:
- Be registered in the Service Account Registry (IT Service Portal)
- Have credentials stored in Vault (vault.internal), never in code or config files
- Have access scoped to the minimum required for their function
- Rotate credentials every 90 days (automated for most services via Vault dynamic secrets)

4.4 Third-Party Data Sharing

Sharing Level 3 or Level 4 data with third parties requires:
- Legal review and executed DPA (Data Processing Agreement) or NDA
- Approval from VP of Legal and data owner
- Security review if third party will store or process the data
- Logging of all data transferred in the Data Transfer Registry

Sending any customer PII to external AI/LLM services (OpenAI, Anthropic,
Google, etc.) is PROHIBITED unless:
- Data is anonymized or pseudonymized first
- A BAA or equivalent agreement is in place with the vendor
- Legal and Security have reviewed and approved the use case

==============================================================
5. PII HANDLING REQUIREMENTS
==============================================================

5.1 PII Categories

The following are considered PII at Meridian:
- Name (full name)
- Email address
- Phone number
- Physical address
- Date of birth
- Government ID numbers (SSN, passport, etc.)
- Payment card information
- IP addresses (when linked to individual)
- Device identifiers (when linked to individual)
- Biometric data
- Location data with precision <= 1km
- Audio or video recordings of individuals

5.2 PII in Engineering Systems

PROHIBITED:
- PII in logs (use user_id or anonymized tokens instead)
- PII in error messages or stack traces
- PII in analytics events (use anonymized identifiers)
- PII in test data (use synthetic data generators)
- PII in RAG training or ingestion pipelines without Legal approval

If you discover PII in a system where it should not be, report immediately
to secops@meridian.com and your manager. Do not delete without guidance
as deletion may itself be a compliance issue.

5.3 PII Detection Tools

Automated PII scanning is available via:
- The Data Platform API: POST /v2/privacy/scan (returns PII confidence scores)
- CI pre-commit hook: runs presidio-based PII detection on log outputs
- Weekly scheduled scans on S3 buckets and GCS buckets (configured by default)

==============================================================
6. AI AND LLM-SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS
==============================================================

6.1 Data in AI Pipelines

All AI/ML pipelines ingesting internal data must:
- Document data sources in the ML Platform Data Catalog
- Apply appropriate anonymization before ingesting PII
- Obtain approval from the Data Governance Committee for Level 4 data
- Log all data ingested with timestamps for audit purposes

6.2 RAG Pipeline Governance

RAG pipelines that expose internal data through LLM interfaces must:
- Restrict retrieval to data the querying user has access to (access-controlled retrieval)
- Not cache retrieved results beyond the session
- Log all queries and retrieved document IDs for 30 days for audit purposes
- Implement guardrails to prevent exfiltration of Restricted data through LLM output

6.3 Model Training Data

Data used to fine-tune or train models is subject to the same classification
and retention rules as the source data. Additionally:
- Training data must be versioned and documented in the ML Platform Data Catalog
- Data lineage must be traceable (source → preprocessing → training dataset)
- Synthetic data generated to replace PII must be documented as synthetic

==============================================================
7. INCIDENT REPORTING
==============================================================

Data breaches or suspected unauthorized access to Level 3 or Level 4 data
must be reported within 1 hour of discovery to:
- secops@meridian.com
- Your direct manager
- The Data Governance Committee DL: data-governance@meridian.com

Response to confirmed breaches will follow the Data Breach Response Playbook
(Confluence: Legal > Data Breach Response).

GDPR breach notification: If EU resident data is involved, Legal must be
notified within 24 hours. Notification to supervisory authority may be
required within 72 hours.

==============================================================
8. POLICY EXCEPTIONS
==============================================================

Exceptions to this policy require written approval from the Data Governance
Committee and the VP of Legal. Exception requests are submitted through
the IT Service Portal. All approved exceptions are logged and subject to
annual review.

==============================================================
9. TRAINING AND ACKNOWLEDGMENT
==============================================================

All employees and contractors must complete the Data Governance Training
module annually via the Learning Management System (LMS). Completion is
tracked and non-compliance is escalated to HR.
